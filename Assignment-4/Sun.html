The Sun

  1. The sun is a massive ball of hot plasma that serves as the central star of our solar system
  2. It is approximately 4.6 billion years old and will continue to shine for about 5 billion more years
  3. The sun is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, with nuclear fusion in its core producing energy.
  4. This energy travels through space as light and heat, making life on Earth possible.
  5. The sun's surface, called the photosphere, has a temperature of about 5,500 degrees Celsius.
  6. Solar flares and coronal mass ejections are powerful eruptions on the sun's surface that can impact Earth's magnetic field.
  7. The sun's gravity is so strong that it keeps all the planets, moons, and other objects in the solar system in orbit.
  8. Light from the sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth, traveling at the speed of light.
  9. Without the sun, Earth's temperatures would plummet, and the planet would become uninhabitable.
  10. Solar energy, harnessed through technologies like solar panels, is a renewable and clean source of power for humanity
